About Game 2048

2048 is a video game, originally a browser-based game, written by 19-year-old Italian developer Gabriele Cirulli in the JavaScript programming language. The game is a numerical puzzle with a field in the form of a square 4x4 cells. The goal of the game is to make tiles with numbers to get a tile of denomination "2048", hence the name (but you can continue playing).

The game starts on a square field of 16 cells, where two cells are occupied by tiles of denominations "2" and "4". Each tile can be moved horizontally or vertically. Each time a player moves a tile, an additional tile of denomination "2" (with 90% probability) or "4" (with 10% probability) appears on the field. Two tiles of the same face value, being placed on the same cell, merge into one, the face value of which is equal to the sum of the merged ones. If there are more than two tiles of the same denomination in one of the lines after the movement, they merge automatically. Each denomination corresponds to its own color, the more denomination, the more "hot" color. For each merge game points are increased by the face value of the newly formed tile. The goal is to get a "2048" tile, after which it is allowed to extend. The game ends if it is impossible to merge tiles after another move.
